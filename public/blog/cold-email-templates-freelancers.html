Cold email is still one of the most effective ways to land freelance clients — when done right. The problem is most freelancers do it wrong: generic subject lines, long emails, zero personalization, and no follow-up.

These 7 templates fix every one of those mistakes. They're short, specific, and designed to convert. Adapt them to your business and the specific problems of each prospect.

How to use these templates: Replace every [bracketed field] with information specific to the business you're contacting. The more specific you are, the higher your response rate. These templates are starting points — personalization is what converts.

The anatomy of a high-converting cold email

Before the templates, understand the formula that makes them work:

  • Subject line: References their specific problem, not your service
  • Opener: Shows you actually looked at their business (1 sentence)
  • Problem: Names the issue and its consequence (1–2 sentences)
  • Solution: What you do, briefly (1 sentence)
  • Social proof: A result you've gotten for a similar business (1 sentence)
  • CTA: A small, low-friction ask — never "buy my service now"

Total: under 120 words. Short emails get read. Long emails get deleted.

Template 1: Web Designer / Developer

Web Design Cold Email Web Design
Subject: [Business name]'s website is losing you customers Hi [First name / Owner], I was checking out [Business name]'s website and noticed it's taking over [X] seconds to load — Google says anything over 3 seconds loses about 40% of visitors before the page even opens. I'm a web designer based in [your city] who rebuilds sites like yours so they load fast, look professional on phones, and actually show up on Google. I helped [similar business type] in [city] get their load time under 2 seconds and their inquiry calls went up about 30% the next month. Would a 15-minute call this week make sense to see if I can do the same for you? — [Your name]

Template 2: SEO Consultant

SEO Cold Email SEO

Ads Cold Email Ads

Template 6: Local SEO / Google Maps

Local SEO Cold Email Local SEO

Incomplete profiles rank significantly lower in local map searches — which means customers physically nearby can't find you. I set up and optimize Google Business Profiles for [niche] in [region]. A properly optimized profile typically doubles the number of "direction requests" and calls within 60 days. Takes me one week to set up, and the results are permanent. Want me to walk you through what's missing on yours? [Your name]

Template 7: Universal Follow-up Email (Day 5)

Follow-up Email — Day 5 Follow-up
Subject: Re: [Original subject line] Hi [First name], Just circling back on my email from a few days ago. Quick thought: I noticed [businesses in your niche] in [city] that invest in [your service] typically see a [specific result — e.g., "20–30% more inbound calls within 90 days"]. Given that [Business name] already has a strong customer base, the upside is probably significant. Still happy to do a quick free [audit / review / analysis] if you'd like to see exactly what we'd focus on. [Your name]

💡 Follow-up rules: Send follow-up #1 on day 3–5. Follow-up #2 on day 10–12 (add social proof). Follow-up #3 on day 18–20 (create light urgency). Final "breakup" email on day 28–30. After that — move on. Don't badger people.

30 subject lines that get opened

Subject lines make or break cold email. Here are 30 proven ones — use the most relevant for each prospect:

  • "[Business name] doesn't appear on Google Maps — here's why"

  • "[Business name] is missing from [platform]"
  • "The reason [Business name]'s site isn't ranking"
  • "One change that could get [Business name] 30% more calls"
  • "[Business name] → [competitor] on Google page 1 (a comparison)"
  • "I analyzed [Business name]'s website. Here's what I found."
  • "Something I noticed about [Business name]'s online presence"
  • "[Business name]'s website is costing you [X] customers/month"
  • "Your competitor just launched a new site. Thoughts?"
  • "[City] businesses using [service] are outpacing those that don't"
  • "Quick [service] question for [Business name]"
  • "I was in [area] and noticed [Business name]..."
  • "Does [Business name] have a way to capture online leads?"
  • "[Business name]'s site loads in [X]s — [Competitor]'s loads in [Y]s"
  • "The 15-minute fix that could help [Business name] rank better"
  • "Re: [Business name]'s Google ranking"
  • "[Business name] + [your service] = quick question"
